# BlackSE: Human Systems Exploitation

> **"I don't look for vulnerabilities in code; I look for them in the people who write it."**

This repository is a curated collection of real-world Social Engineering (SE) engagements conducted by **Jake Lo**. 

With over 12 years of experience in high-stakes Marketing and Sales, I have spent over a decade mastering the art of persuasion, psychological triggers, and human behavior. While becoming a "hacker" was always my dream, I eventually reached two realizations that defined my path:

1. **Efficiency Over Complexity:** I am not a traditional "technical guy." I have little interest in memorizing complex syntax or learning low-level languages when there is a faster way.
2. **The Human Gateway:** I discovered that I could often gain full access to a target while technical hackers were still performing their initial Nmap scans.

In this repo, I document real cases where I exploited human systems without the targets ever realizing they were being "hacked." These actions were conducted ethically, driven by a desire to explore the unseen mechanics of our world. No harm was done; no damage was inflicted. This is simply a glimpse into how a hacker navigates daily life.

### The "Spider-Sense" Approach

I do not use academic jargon or textbook definitions. When you have spent a lifetime studying the human system, these "professional terms" become invisible—they are simply part of my intuition, my "spider-sense." 

This repository is dedicated to **Red Teamers** and fellow practitioners who see the world through the same lens. I am sharing these cases to show you that you are not alone in this craft.

### Repository Philosophy

* **No Theory:** You won't find definitions of "Phishing" or "Pretexting" here. 
* **Pure Execution:** Every entry is a real case study of a human exploit.
* **Ethics:** All engagements are documented for educational and security-awareness purposes.

If you find value in these field notes, **Star this repo** to support the work.
